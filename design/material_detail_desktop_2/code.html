Advanced Graph Algorithms and Network Flow Variations

Dr. Elena Rostova

Dept. of Computer Science

calendar_today Published: Oct 12, 2023

Abstract

This comprehensive document details advanced concepts in graph theory as applied to modern computing architectures. We explore the nuanced variations of the Max-Flow Min-Cut theorem, focusing on its application in distributed networks and routing optimization. The notes include detailed algorithmic breakdowns of Dinic's algorithm and push-relabel methods, accompanied by time complexity proofs and practical implementations in Python and C++.

Furthermore, the material bridges theoretical concepts with real-world applications in bipartite matching problems and scheduling. Suitable for graduate-level students or advanced undergraduates preparing for technical interviews or systems research.
